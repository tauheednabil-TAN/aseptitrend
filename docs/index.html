AseptiTrend

A proactive environmental-monitoring (EM) trending and excursion assistant for aseptic filling. It sets data-driven limits against EU GMP Annex 1, catches the excursions and slow drifts a spreadsheet misses, and drafts a first-pass investigation note for a human to review.

Why I built this

Taking the repetitive part of EM trending off the team's plate

Environmental-monitoring trending is often manual and spreadsheet-bound: an analyst re-derives alert and action limits, eyeballs charts location by location, and can miss a gradual drift that never trips a single-point limit until it finally breaches. I don't have production EM hours yet, so rather than claim experience I don't have, I built a working tool that does that first pass — computing limits, screening every series against the applicable rules, and ranking what needs a human's attention.

Regulated-industry framing. Because EM work in pharma has to be auditable, I treated every part of this as if it had to survive review: limits are capped against the EU GMP Annex 1 (2022) ceilings, flags are append-only records with UTC timestamps, and a human reviews everything — the tool never makes a batch, release, or quality decision.

Trend detection

Limits drawn in, excursions caught

Each location and sample type is charted with its alert, action, and EU GMP Annex 1 specification limits drawn in. Data-driven limits (90th / 95th percentile) come from the historical baseline; the recent monitoring window is screened against them.

Grade A is "no growth". Any viable recovery of ≥1 CFU is an immediate action-level event, regardless of statistics — shown here as a single 2 CFU/m³ recovery flagged on an otherwise flat Grade A line.

Excursion flags & AI draft

The drift a spreadsheet hides — and a first-draft investigation

Beyond single-point breaches, AseptiTrend detects adverse trends: a gradual climb toward the action limit that never trips a single-point rule until it breaches. Every flag is an append-only record with full context, and one click drafts an investigation note for a human to review and complete.

Human-in-the-loop. The AI (Google Gemini, called server-side) drafts the note — summary, areas to review, immediate checks — but never decides disposition, release, or CAPA outcome. Every draft is labelled "AI-generated draft — for human review only."

Data & synthetic model

Reproducible synthetic data, or bring your own

The app runs on a seeded, reproducible synthetic dataset — ~90 days across all four grades with deliberately injected storylines so the tool has something real to find. Results can also be imported from CSV, validated and normalised on the way in.

How it maps to the role

Practice mapped to an EM analyst's work

  • Trend analysis Data-driven alert (P90) and action (P95) limits, capped below the Annex 1 ceiling and recomputed whenever new results arrive.
  • Excursion management Five rules — single-point breaches, consecutive-rise trends, repeated alerts, and the Grade A no-growth rule — each hit an append-only record.
  • Regulatory limits EU GMP Annex 1 (2022) maxima for Grades A–D, viable and non-viable, hardcoded as the governing specification ceiling.
  • Investigation support AI-drafted investigation notes that structure the human's review — never a quality, disposition, or release decision.
  • Reducing manual work Automates the repetitive first pass of EM trending so the team spends time on judgement, not re-deriving limits.
  • Engineering Next.js + TypeScript, unit-tested statistics and flagging logic (44 passing tests), deployed on Vercel.

This is a personal portfolio project on synthetic data — not validated, not GMP-qualified, and it never makes a batch, release, or quality decision. It reflects how I prepare: by building the real workflow in real tools rather than only reading about it. I'm keen to apply and extend this in a live EM environment.
